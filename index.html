The Whispering Gallery
Lower level of Grand Central Terminal, at the intersection of the walkways. Stand in diagonally opposite corners of the arched ceiling — face away from each other and whisper. Despite the crowds, only your partner hears you. Free, takes 5 minutes, completely magical.

A 1916 Italian Renaissance villa on Biscayne Bay — one of Miami's most beautiful and least-touristed spots. Formal gardens descend to the water, the interiors are extraordinary. Allow 2 hours. Almost no one in the honeymooning-in-Miami guides mentions this — which is exactly why it's worth going.

Before you go

Things to know

Book these immediately
  • The French Laundry — books out months ahead. Go to thomaskeller.com now.
  • Alila Ventana Big Sur — adults-only, limited rooms, May peak season.
  • Auberge du Soleil — Michelin Three Key, fills fast.
  • Opus One + Domaine Carneros — winery reservations required.
  • State Bird Provisions + Californios (San Francisco) — book 2–3 weeks out.
  • Maverick Helicopters — Grand Canyon sunset tour, sells out in May.
Fuel — the only real rule
  • Big Sur: Full tank in Carmel before leaving. No fuel on Highway 1. Non-negotiable.
  • San Francisco: Top up before heading north to Redwoods.
  • Oregon border: Fuel at Crescent City. Limited ahead.
  • Never go below half tank on Big Sur or the Oregon coast.
  • Avoid electric vehicles — charging infrastructure is too sparse on this route.
Honeymoon extras
  • Mention "honeymoon stay" at every hotel — upgrades, champagne, and room touches are common courtesy at these properties.
  • At Alila Ventana: ask specifically for a suite with private hot tub + ocean view.
  • At Auberge du Soleil: request a maison with private terrace and valley view.
  • At The French Laundry: note one vegetarian guest — they'll customise the tasting menu.
  • At L'Auberge Carmel: book Aubergine through the hotel directly.
